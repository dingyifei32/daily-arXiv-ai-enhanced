### [1] [Comparing Hemispheres: Anisotropy in the deceleration parameter $q_0$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03004)
*Mauricio Lopez-Hernandez,Josue De-Santiago*

Main category: astro-ph.CO

TL;DR: 本文利用Pantheon+超新星样本对减速参数$q_0$进行半球比较分析，测试宇宙加速的各向同性。研究发现即使在经过完全运动学修正的$z_{\mathrm{HD}}$参考系中，$q_0$仍存在残余偶极各向异性（$Δq_0=0.112$，信噪比2.155），方向与CMB偶极子对齐。超新星数据推断出的偶极子速度与Planck CMB结果存在~1.9σ差异。将超新星推断的偶极子纳入红移修正后，各向异性被抑制，表明信号部分源于局部速度场建模的残余不匹配，暗示存在未被完全捕获的残余体流。

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 检验宇宙加速是否各向同性，以及当前红移修正（特别是CMB偶极子和本动速度修正）的鲁棒性，探究Pantheon+数据中是否存在残余的各向异性信号及其来源。

Method: 使用Pantheon+ Ia型超新星样本，在三种红移框架（$z_{\mathrm{hel}}$、$z_{\mathrm{CMB}}$、$z_{\mathrm{HD}}$）下进行半球比较分析，计算减速参数$q_0$的偶极各向异性。通过改变最小红移截止值测试信号稳定性，并利用超新星数据自身推断偶极子方向与速度，将其纳入修正流程以检验各向异性来源。

Result: 1）$z_{\mathrm{HD}}$框架下仍有残余偶极各向异性：$Δq_0=0.112$，最大S/N=2.155，方向与CMB偶极子对齐，且随最小红移增大而减弱；2）$z_{\mathrm{hel}}$和$z_{\mathrm{CMB}}$框架中各向异性更强；3）超新星推断的偶极子速度$v_{\odot}=307.26^{+32.00}_{-22.28}$ km/s，方向(RA,DEC)=(156.40°, -3.38°)，与Planck CMB结果存在~1.9σ差异；4）将超新星推断偶极子纳入修正后，偶极模式消失，S/N降至≤1.75，剩余波动与统计噪声一致。

Abstract: We present a hemispherical comparison analysis of the deceleration parameter $q_0$ using the Pantheon+ sample of Type Ia supernovae to test the isotropy of cosmic acceleration and the robustness of redshift corrections. We detect directional variations in $q_0$ across redshift frames. Even in the $z_{\mathrm{HD}}$ frame, where corrections for the CMB dipole and peculiar velocities are applied, a residual dipolar anisotropy persists with $Δq_0 = 0.112$ and a maximum signal to noise $S/N = 2.155$, aligned with the CMB dipole direction and decreasing with increasing minimum redshift cut.   <summary>Details</summary>

Method: 在假设注入时标远小于视界的前提下，推导了标量、矢量和张量扰动下光子测地线的一般修正，并通过积分Sachs-Wolfe效应计算对CMB的影响。

Abstract: We develop a formalism to characterize the imprints of late-time sources of cosmological fluctuations under the sole assumption that the injection occurs on timescales short compared to the horizon. For post-recombination injections, we derive the general modification of photon geodesics in the presence of scalar, vector, and tensor perturbations, and compute the resulting impact on the Cosmic Microwave Background through the integrated Sachs-Wolfe effect. We show that the signal is generically dominated by instantaneous injections of anisotropic stress. As an application, we consider first-order phase transitions in a sequestered dark sector and show that current observations constrain fractional energy injections at the permille level.
