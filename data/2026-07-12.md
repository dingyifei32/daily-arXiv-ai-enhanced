### [1] [Multi-Wavelength Signatures of a Giant Cometary Radio Halo in MACSJ0417-1154](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07750)
*Ramananda Santra,Marco Balboni,Ruta Kale*

Main category: astro-ph.HE

TL;DR: 本文首次对星系团MACSJ0417（z=0.445）中的射电晕进行了多频研究，结合uGMRT、MeerKAT和XMM-Newton数据，发现射电晕延伸约1.75 Mpc，两个候选遗迹位于2.9 Mpc处。射电晕和遗迹的积分谱符合单一幂律（谱指数分别为α≈-1.3和α≈-1.6）。研究揭示了射电表面亮度边缘与X射线不连续性的空间重合，以及射电与X射线表面亮度之间的强相关性（相关系数~0.85）。结果支持湍流再加速模型，排除了纯强子模型，并提出该星系团正经历一个质量比~6:1的离轴并合事件，驱动湍流从而产生巨型射电晕。

Motivation: 星系团中的弥漫非热射电辐射能直接揭示星系团内介质（ICM）的等离子体过程，而MACSJ0417作为一个包含射电晕和候选遗迹的星系团，尚未进行过多频段联合研究，作者希望通过对该天体的多频观测来理解其非热辐射的物理机制和起源。

Method: 使用uGMRT（300-850 MHz）和MeerKAT（900-1670 MHz）进行射电观测，结合XMM-Newton的X射线档案数据。通过成像得到射电晕和遗迹的形态及延伸范围，计算积分谱和空间分辨的谱指数图，分析射电与X射线表面亮度的相关性，并结合X射线温度分布进行动力学解读。

Result: （1）射电晕延伸约1.75 Mpc（400 MHz），两候选遗迹R1和R2位于2.9 Mpc处；（2）射电晕和遗迹的积分谱符合单一幂律，谱指数分别为α≈-1.3和α≈-1.6；（3）发现射电表面亮度边缘（距星系团中心~43″东南方向）与X射线不连续性一致；（4）400-1280 MHz分辨谱指数图显示显著波动和径向陡化；（5）X射线呈东南-西北延伸形态，沿该轴存在高温区（~11 keV）；（6）射电与X射线表面亮度强相关（相关系数~0.85），且相关斜率从400 MHz的次线性演变为1280 MHz的线性。

Conclusion: 观测结果支持湍流再加速模型，而纯强子模型因宇宙线质子能量需求不现实而被排除。作者提出MACSJ0417正经历一个质量比~6:1的离轴解体并合（沿东南-西北方向），该并合保留了冷核，同时驱动湍流为巨型射电晕提供能量。

Abstract: Galaxy clusters hosting diffuse non-thermal radio emission offer direct insight into plasma processes of the intracluster medium (ICM). We present the first multi-frequency study of the radio halo in MACSJ0417 (z = 0.445) using uGMRT (300-850 MHz), MeerKAT (900-1670 MHz), and archival \textit{XMM-Newton} data. The halo extends to $\sim$1.75 Mpc at 400 MHz, while two candidate relics (R1 and R2) are detected at 2.9 Mpc. The integrated spectra follow single power-laws with spectral indices $α\simeq -1.3$ for the halo and $α\simeq -1.6$ for the relics. Sensitive uGMRT imaging reveals a radio surface brightness edge $\sim$43$''$ SE of the cluster centre, which coincides with an X-ray discontinuity. Resolved spectral maps (400--1280 MHz) show significant fluctuations and a clear radial steepening of the spectral index. X-ray analysis reveals an elongated SE-NW morphology and high-temperature regions ($\sim$11 keV) along this axis. A strong radio and X-ray surface brightness correlation is found (correlation coefficient $\sim$ 0.85), with the correlation slope evolving from sublinear at 400 MHz to linear at 1280 MHz. These results, together with the spectral properties, support the turbulent re-acceleration model and point to inhomogeneous ICM conditions. The pure hadronic model is excluded owing to unrealistic energy requirements for cosmic-ray protons. We propose that MACSJ0417 is undergoing a minor off-axis dissociative merger (mass ratio $\sim$6:1) along the SE-NW axis, which has preserved its cool core while driving turbulence that powers the giant radio halo.
